Welcome to Cambodia, a vibrant and enchanting country that is the perfect holiday destination for women looking for a unique and adventurous experience! From the stunning temples of Angkor Wat to the bustling streets of Phnom Penh, Cambodia has something for everyone. Whether you're interested in exploring ancient ruins, trying delicious street food, or simply soaking up the sun on a white sandy beach, Cambodia has it all. As a woman traveling in Cambodia, you'll find a warm and welcoming culture that is both safe and respectful. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in one of Southeast Asia's most exciting destinations!

There are many cultural customs to be aware of in Cambodia. Here is some brief information about what to expect, as well as some customs to be aware of in Cambodia.

  • The food is a must try in Cambodia! With flavours similar to its Southeast Asian neighbours Thailand and Vietnam, you can expect bursts of flavour in street foods and stalls. Although meat is consumed widely throughout the country, it is possible to find Buddhist vegetarian cuisine as well as halal food places owned by Cham Cambodians, who practice Islam. If you have allergies, please exercise caution as cross contamination can be an issue, and be especially careful at street food stalls as hygiene standards are not as robust as other nations.
  • The national religion of Cambodia is Theravada Buddhism, which influences many aspects of daily life. Beautiful, picturesque temples are dotted all over the country and are worth visiting, especially UNESCO World Heritage Site Angkor Wat in Siem Reap. Monks are highly respected in Cambodian society, and its common for young men to spend some time in a monastery as a rite of passage. It is important to know that it is polite etiquette to show respect to monks, with women to avoid physical contact with them.
  • Cambodia is a conservative society, with high emphasis on family, religion and tradition. Public displays of affection, revealing clothing especially at temples is to be avoided. When visiting someone's home or temple, remember to remove your shoes before entering.
  • The language of Cambodia is Khmer! It is worth learning a few phrases to help you out, and the locals will appreciate your effort. Don't worry if you are struggling though, there are many places, especially in the capital Phnom Penh, where locals speak English.

  1. Avoid walking alone at night: It's generally not recommended to walk alone at night, especially in areas that are unfamiliar to you. Stick to well-lit and busy areas and consider using transportation such as taxis or tuk-tuks.
  2. Use reputable transportation: If you're taking a taxi or tuk-tuk, make sure to use a reputable company or have your hotel or hostel arrange transportation for you. Avoid taking unmarked or unregistered vehicles.
  3. Stay in a safe accommodation: Choose accommodation that is located in a safe and well-lit area. Look for hotels or hostels that have good reviews, secure entrances, and 24-hour staff.
  4. Avoid excessive alcohol consumption: Drinking too much alcohol can impair your judgment and make you more vulnerable to unsafe situations. Drink responsibly and be mindful of your surroundings.
  5. Be aware of scams: Be cautious of scams that target tourists, such as tuk-tuk drivers who offer to take you to "secret" temples or shops. Stick to reputable tour operators and do your research before committing to any deals.
  6. Trust your instincts: If you feel uncomfortable in a situation, trust your instincts and remove yourself from the situation. Don't be afraid to ask for help from locals or seek assistance from your hotel or hostel staff.

There are limited flight options in to Cambodia. Check with your airline about requirements to board flights.

COVID-19 remains a health problem in Cambodia. Exercise basic hygiene and plan ahead with health insurance. The government has directed the use of face masks and sanitisers, especially in public areas. You may also be asked to provide proof of vaccination in some venues in Phnom Penh.

Make sure you bring medications you require, as it may be difficult to find what you need in Cambodia. Keep up to date with travel vaccinations also.

It is ideal to travel outside monsoon season, which runs from June to October, as flash floods are common, and can cause major traffic congestion in big cities.
