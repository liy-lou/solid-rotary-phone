Visit Morocco



A country that forms part of Africa.



Travelling to Morocco as a female traveller can be an exciting and rewarding experience, but it's important to be aware of the cultural and social norms of the country.



Culture

Morocco is a conservative society, and as such, female travellers should dress modestly, especially in public places. It's recommended to wear loose-fitting clothing that covers the arms and legs, and to avoid revealing clothing such as shorts, tank tops, or low-cut tops. While it's not necessary to wear a hijab or headscarf, it's best to respect local customs and dress appropriately.



Advice

Female travellers should also be cautious when walking alone at night and void isolated areas. However, Morocco is generally a safe country, and female travellers should not be discouraged from exploring all that this beautiful country has to offer.





    Travel tips and customs

  • Morocco is a popular tourist destination with diverse attractions, including stunning beaches, beautiful architecture, vibrant markets, and the Sahara Desert.
  • Morocco has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Berber, Arab, and European influences. Visitors can explore traditional souks, taste delicious Moroccan cuisine, and experience local festivals and celebrations.
  • Morocco is a safe country to travel to, with friendly locals and a well-developed tourism infrastructure. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid petty crime, and women travellers should dress modestly and be aware of cultural norms.
