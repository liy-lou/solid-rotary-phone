Visit Morocco. A country that forms part of Africa. Morocco is a popular tourist desination with diverse attractions, including stunning beaches, beautiful architecture, vibrant markets and the Sahara Desert. With a rich cultural heritage made up of Berber, Arab, and European influence, travellers can find themselves exploring traditional souks, delicious Moroccon cuisine and local festivals.

There are many cultural customs to be aware of in Morocco. Here is some brief information about what to expect, as well as some customs to be aware of in Morocco.

  • In Morocco, you can expect to experience a fascinating blend of ancient and modern, traditional and cosmopolitan. From exploring bustling medinas and vibrant markets to savoring delicious tagines and mint tea, Morocco offers a vibrant and unforgettable sensory experience.
  • In Morocco, it is customary to greet people with a handshake and to use your right hand for eating and handling items. Additionally, it is important to dress modestly and respectfully, especially in more conservative areas or when visiting mosques or religious sites.
  • When shopping in Morocco, it is customary to haggle over prices, as this is seen as a normal part of the shopping experience. It is also important to remove your shoes when entering a Moroccan home or mosque, as wearing shoes indoors is considered impolite.
  • Another important custom to be aware of in Morocco is to ask for permission before taking someone's picture, especially in more traditional or rural areas. Some people may be uncomfortable with having their picture taken, and it is important to respect their wishes and privacy.

  1. While Morocco is generally a safe country, it is important to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded tourist areas where pickpocketing and other petty crimes can occur.
  2. Women traveling alone may experience unwanted attention, so it is important to dress modestly and consider traveling with a group or a trusted guide.
  3. It is advisable to avoid political demonstrations and large public gatherings, as they can sometimes turn violent.
  4. When driving in Morocco, it is important to be alert and cautious, as road conditions can be unpredictable and traffic laws may not always be followed.
  5. It is recommended to drink bottled water and avoid tap water to prevent the risk of waterborne illnesses.
  6. If you plan to hike or explore remote areas, it is important to go with a reputable guide or group and to inform someone of your itinerary beforehand in case of emergencies.

The entry requirements for tourists to Morocco include presenting a negative COVID-19 PCR test taken within 72 hours prior to arrival, completing a health questionnaire, and submitting to a health screening upon arrival.
